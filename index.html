Who is George Orwell?

While deeply opposing some socialist states in real life, he was a democratic socialist himself and deeply opposed totalitarianism and authoritarianism. He fought in the spanish civil war on the socialist side, but when he saw the distortion the press was doing in favor of the socialists, he was dramatically affected. George Orwell, or Eric Arthur Blair, was mainly characterized by his social critiques and commentary and his books, Nineteen Eighty-Four, Animal Farm, The Road to Wigan Pier, and Homage to Catalonia.

Intro to Nineteen Eighty-Four

Nineteen Eighty-four is a very complex book, about a man as he tries to rebel against an authoritarian government, while also working for said government. As the protagonist makes allies and foes, he starts to rebel against the regime more and more publicly.

Theme and Symbol

While there are many themes in Nineteen Eighty-Four, the most important (subjectively) is the extreme poverty and inequality shown throughout the entire book. Even though the party says that life was better now more so than then, it is not shown that way, with the proles living in hunger, thirst, disease, filth, while the leaders at the top live in grandeur. Another prevalent theme is the surveillance, which is also omnipresent in the book. The upper and middle classes all have telescreens to be watched and observed with. Citizens are so tightly controlled, that even the slightest sign of doubt can lead to death.
